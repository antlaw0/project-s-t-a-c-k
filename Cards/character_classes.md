# Project S.T.A.C.K.
## Starting Character Class Cards
### Version 0.1 — First Playtest Roster
### June 28, 2026

> These five Character Cards establish the default first-playtest roster for the Medieval Fantasy module. They use the current core rules: 5 Skill Slots, 5 Tactical Reserve slots, shared activations with Controlled Allies, independent Autonomous Ally activations, the shared 5-space Status Row, weapon range and subtype eligibility, and the current HP values.
>
> Each card includes a **starter loadout reference**. The referenced equipment, Skills, Spells, Abilities, and Tactical Reserve cards are separate cards. The Character Card itself does not occupy an Equipment, Skill, Tactical Reserve, Inventory, or Status Row slot.

---

# 1. Starter Roster Conventions

## 1.1 What Every Character Card Provides

Every player character begins with:

- **5 Skill Slots**.
- **5 Tactical Reserve slots**.
- **20 Inventory capacity**.
- **1 Transportation slot**.
- Access to all normal equipment slots.
- A personal **5-space Status Row**.
- One **shared activation** each Encounter Round, shared with that character's Controlled Allies.

A player chooses the character's actual starting row when entering a dungeon. The “Suggested First Placement” line is advice, not a restriction.

## 1.2 Native Skill Domains

The domains below establish the default first-module taxonomy. A Skill Card whose domain appears on a Character Card is a **Native Skill** for that character.

| Class | Native Skill Domains |
| --- | --- |
| Warrior | Barbarian, Vanguard, Marksman |
| Rogue | Thief, Skirmisher, Shadowcraft |
| Mage | Elementalism, Arcana, Necromancy |
| Cleric | Restoration, Divine Intervention, Exorcism |
| Druid | Biomancy, Wildcalling, Primalism |

A character may equip a Native Skill at a safe zone under the normal Native Skill rules. These domains do not prevent future class expansions from adding new domains.

## 1.3 Starter-Loadout Rule

The listed starter cards are the **recommended default first-playtest loadout**. A scenario may replace them, but should not give a character a stronger starting package without testing that change deliberately.

Each starter character begins with **2 rank-1 Skill Cards**, each holding 1 matching rank-1 Spell or Ability Card. The character therefore begins with 2 occupied Skill Slots and 3 open Skill Slots.

Unless a scenario says otherwise, each starter character begins with the 3 indicated Tactical Reserve cards and leaves 2 Tactical Reserve slots empty. This keeps the first test focused while leaving space to test newly acquired consumables.

# 2. Warrior Character Card

```text
WARRIOR
Player Character — Martial

Maximum HP: 20
Suggested First Placement: Player Front Row

Class Trait — Weapon Mastery
The Warrior's melee weapon attacks ignore 1 Defense.
[I'm worried this is too OP]
Native Skill Domains
Barbarian; knight; Marksman.

Starting Equipment
• Iron Arming Sword
  Melee Weapon — Sword — One-Handed
  Weapon Attack: Deal 3 Physical damage.
• Buckler
  +1 Defense.
• Leather Armor
  +1 Defense.

Starting Skills and Attached Abilities
• Recruit Barbarian
  Attached Ability: Rage
• Recruit Vanguard
  Attached Ability: Shield Bash

Starting Tactical Reserve
• Minor Healing Potion
• Bandage
• Antidote

Starting Defense: 2
```

### Warrior Role and First-Test Intent

The Warrior is the baseline frontline character. It tests physical Defense, high HP, melee range restrictions, a shield, Heat accumulation through repeated attacks, and the decision to spend a shared activation on the Warrior rather than an owned Controlled Ally.

### Required Starter Companion Card Text

```text
Rage
Ability — Barbarian — Recruit
Action — Persistent Positive Effect

Use the Warrior's shared activation. Place this card in the Warrior's Status Row.

While Rage is active, the Warrior's melee weapon attacks deal +1 Physical damage.

At the end of any later Encounter Round in which the Warrior did not deal damage during its shared activation, Rage ends.

When Rage ends, return this card to Recruit Barbarian.
```
```text
Shield Bash
Ability — Vanguard — Recruit
Action
Requires: A shield and a melee weapon.

Make a melee weapon attack with -1 damage.
If that attack deals 1 or more damage, the target gains Weakened.
```

# 3. Rogue Character Card

```text
ROGUE
Player Character — Martial

Maximum HP: 18
Suggested First Placement: Player Front Row

Class Trait — Shadow Step
Once per Encounter Round, immediately before or after the Rogue
uses its own normal action, move the Rogue to an empty Player Front
Row or Player Back Row slot. This movement does not spend an action.

Native Skill Domains
Thief; Skirmisher; Shadowcraft.

Starting Equipment
• Main-Gauche
  Melee Weapon — Dagger — One-Handed
  Weapon Attack: Deal 2 Physical damage.
• Leather Armor
  +1 Defense.

Starting Skills and Attached Abilities
• Novice Thief
  Attached Ability: Opportunist's Strike
• Novice Skirmisher
  Attached Ability: Knife Throw

Starting Tactical Reserve
• Minor Healing Potion
• Smoke Bomb
• Antidote

Starting Defense: 1
```

### Rogue Role and First-Test Intent

The Rogue tests row changes, positional play, Dagger subtype requirements, a Front Row melee attack, and a small independent ranged option. Shadow Step intentionally works only when the Rogue takes its own normal action. It does not let the Rogue reposition for free when a Controlled Ally is selected to use the group’s shared activation.

### Required Starter Companion Card Text

```text
Opportunist's Strike
Ability — Thief — Novice
Action
Requires: A melee weapon with the Dagger tag.

Make a melee weapon attack with +1 damage.
If the target already has 1 or more Damage counters, gain +1 Heat.
```

```text
Knife Throw
Ability — Skirmisher — Novice
Action

Deal 1 Physical damage to 1 enemy in either opposing row.
Keyword: Piercing.

This is a ranged Ability, not a ranged weapon attack.
It does not require an equipped Ranged weapon.
```

```text
MAGE
Player Character — Arcane

Maximum HP: 12
Suggested First Placement: Player Back Row

Class Trait — Spellweaver
The first damaging Spell the Mage casts during its own normal action

Native Skill Domains
Elementalism; Arcana; Necromancy.

Starting Equipment
• Apprentice's Wand
  Held Focus — Arcane
  No weapon attack. This card may satisfy a card requirement for an
  Arcane Focus.
• Apprentice Robe
  Clothing. +0 Defense.

Starting Skills and Attached Spells
• Novice Elementalism
  Attached Spell: Firebolt
• Novice Arcana
  Attached Spell: Arcane Ward

Starting Tactical Reserve
• Minor Healing Potion
• Scroll of Force Dart
• Antidote

Starting Defense: 0
```

### Mage Role and First-Test Intent

The Mage is intentionally fragile but has dependable ranged pressure. Spellweaver applies only to a damaging Spell cast during the Mage’s own normal action. It does not increase damage from a Tactical Reserve scroll, a reaction, a persistent effect, or another Friendly Entity’s attack.

### Required Starter Companion Card Text

```text
Firebolt
Spell — Elementalism — Novice
Action — Spell Attack

Deal 2 Fire damage to 1 enemy in either opposing row.
Gain 1 Heat.
```

```text
Arcane Ward
Spell — Arcana — Novice
Action — Persistent Positive Effect

Place this card in the Mage's Status Row.
The Mage gains +1 Defense until the start of the Mage's next shared activation.

When this effect ends, return this card to Novice Arcana.
```

### Cleric Role and First-Test Intent

The Cleric tests repeatable healing, Heat reduction, a second support-focused action option, and anti-Undead bonus damage without creating a separate Holy damage type. Sacred Relief uses the target's Heat, not the Cleric’s Heat, and cannot reduce Heat below 0.

### Required Starter Companion Card Text


### Druid Role and First-Test Intent

The Druid tests a spell that deals Physical damage, a Controlled Ally, and the shared-activation choice between acting personally or activating the Vine Guardian. Wild Pathfinder will not matter in a dungeon-only test, but it should be retained because it gives the Druid a clear exploration identity from the first campaign session.

### Required Starter Companion Card Text

```text

Spell — Biomancy — Novice
Action — Spell Attack

Keywords: Magical, Piercing.
Gain 1 Heat.
```

```text
Summon — Wildcalling — Novice
Action — Controlled Ally

Summon Requirement: Spend the Druid's shared activation and place
this card in an empty Player Front Row slot.

Maximum HP: 5
Defense: 1

Actions When Selected for the Druid's Shared Activation
• Root Slam: Deal 2 Physical damage to 1 enemy in the opposing Front Row.
• Entangling Roots: Choose 1 enemy in either opposing row.
  That enemy gains Immobilized.

A newly summoned Vine Guardian cannot take a normal action during
the same shared activation used to summon it.
```

# 8. First-Playtest Balance Snapshot

| Character | HP | Starting Defense | Primary Test Role | Major Vulnerability |
| --- | ---: | ---: | --- | --- |
| Warrior | 20 | 2 | Frontline Physical damage and Defense | Limited reach without a ranged option |
| Rogue | 18 | 1 | Positioning, Dagger attacks, flexible target access | Cannot absorb repeated focused attacks |
| Mage | 12 | 0 | High ranged spell damage | Very fragile and Heat-prone |
| Cleric | 14 | 0 | Healing, Heat relief, anti-Undead damage | Low direct damage and low Defense |
| Druid | 14 | 1 | Spell damage, Controlled Ally choice, exploration | Must choose between Druid and summon actions |

## 8.1 Intended Early-Game Power Standard

The starter characters are not intended to be perfectly symmetrical in damage. Their baseline roles are deliberately different:

- The **Warrior** is the most durable direct attacker.
- The **Rogue** is the most mobile martial character.
- The **Mage** has the best repeatable ranged damage but the worst defense.
- The **Cleric** keeps the group operational rather than winning damage races.
- The **Druid** trades one direct action for flexible summon control and map value.

No starter Character Card should start with a permanent hireling, a rank-2 Skill Card, more than 2 attached Spells or Abilities, an active summon already in play, a Defense score above 2, or more than 3 prepared Tactical Reserve cards.

# 9. Print and Setup Checklist

For a first test with all five classes, print:

- 5 Character Cards from this file.
- 10 rank-1 Skill Cards.
- 10 attached starter Spell or Ability Cards.
- 1 Vine Guardian Summon Card.
- 7 shared Tactical Reserve card types, with duplicates for every listed starter copy.
- The listed weapons, armor, shields, robes, and focus equipment as separate cards.
- Damage counters, Heat counters, Status cards, and the Bleed Out sequence.

The Character Cards are designed to work with the existing shared-activation and status-card systems. They should be printed as the anchor cards of each player’s personal area, with Skill Slots, Tactical Reserve slots, Equipment slots, and the Status Row arranged around them in the physical player board or playmat.
