I kept these **generic**, so every discipline can use them, while preserving the special identity of Biomancy, Elementalism, Necromancy, and Thief abilities. The cards are deliberately single-use and mostly immediate, avoiding a second buff ecosystem that would compete with your existing status-row system.

# Starting Tactical Reserve Cards

## Tactical Reserve Rules

### Tactical Reserve Cards

Tactical Reserve cards are consumable items, tools, and scrolls carried into a dungeon.

They do not occupy:

* Skill Card slots
* Ability Card slots
* Spell slots
* Equipment slots
Each player character has five tactical reserve card slots.
Each Tactical Reserve card has one owner. Unless a card says otherwise, only that owner may use it.
Once a tactical reserve card is used, discard it by shuffling it back into the Loot deck so it can appear again in the game.

---

## Tactical Reserve Use Timing

### Action

A card marked **Action** uses the owner's normal shared activation.

If the owner is Stunned, that owner cannot use an Action Tactical Reserve card because the Stunned entity loses its shared activation.

### Reaction

A card marked **Reaction** is used immediately when its listed trigger occurs.

Reaction Tactical Reserve cards do not use the owner's shared activation.

A Stunned player character may still use a Reaction Tactical Reserve card unless the card's own text says otherwise.

### Prepared Dungeon Effect

A card marked **Prepared Dungeon Effect** uses the owner's normal shared activation when cast.

Place the card in the owner's status row. It waits there until its listed Dungeon Deck trigger occurs.

When that trigger occurs, resolve the card's effect, then move the card to the Tactical Reserve Discard Pile.

Prepared Dungeon Effect cards are not positive status effects. They are held in the status row only because that row already provides a stable, tactilely trackable location for active effects.

A Friendly Entity cannot have more than one active copy of the same named Prepared Dungeon Effect.

---

## Scroll Rules

Scrolls are magical Tactical Reserve cards that can be used by any Friendly Entity.

A scroll:

* Does not require a Skill Card.
* Does not require a spell slot.
* Does not count as casting a Biomancy, Elementalism, Necromancy, or other school spell.
* Does not benefit from skill-card passives that modify spells.
* Does not trigger effects that require a school spell to be cast.

A scroll is magical equipment, but it is not an equipped spell.

# Potions and Remedies

## Healing Potion

* **Type:** Tactical Reserve — Potion
* **Use:** Action
* **Target:** Yourself or one ally on the same row
* **Effect:** Restore **2 HP** to the target.

---

## Antitoxin Vial

* **Type:** Tactical Reserve — Potion
* **Use:** Reaction
* **Trigger:** You or one ally on your row gains one or more Poison status cards.
* **Target:** The Friendly Entity that gained Poison
* **Effect:** Remove **one Poison status card** from the target.

Use Antitoxin Vial after the Poison status card is placed in the target's status row.

---

## Burn Salve

* **Type:** Tactical Reserve — Remedy
* **Use:** Action
* **Target:** Yourself or one ally on the same row
* **Effect:** Remove **Burning** from the target.

Then restore **1 HP** to that target.

---

## Coagulant Pack

* **Type:** Tactical Reserve — Remedy
* **Use:** Action
* **Target:** Yourself or one ally on the same row
* **Effect:** Remove **Bleeding** from the target.

Then restore **1 HP** to that target.

---

## Focus Draught

* **Type:** Tactical Reserve — Potion
* **Use:** Reaction
* **Trigger:** You would gain Stunned.
* **Target:** Yourself
* **Effect:** Prevent the Stunned status card from being placed in your status row.

Focus Draught does not remove Stunned after it has already been applied.

---

* **Target:** Yourself

# Combat Tools

## Fire Flask

* **Type:** Tactical Reserve — Thrown Weapon
* **Use:** Action
* **Range:** Ranged
* **Target:** One enemy
* **Effect:** Deal **2 fire damage** to the target.

This damage is not a weapon attack and does not benefit from weapon-attack modifiers.

---

## Acid Flask

* **Type:** Tactical Reserve — Thrown Weapon
* **Use:** Action
* **Range:** Ranged
* **Target:** One enemy
* **Effect:** Deal **1 physical damage** to the target.

Then place Acid Flask in that enemy's status row.

The target has **-1 Defense** until the end of the current round.

At the end of the current round, move Acid Flask to shuffle back into Loot deck.

---

## Smoke Bomb

* **Type:** Tactical Reserve — Combat Tool
* **Use:** Reaction
* **Trigger:** An enemy attack would deal damage to you.
* **Target:** Yourself
* **Effect:** Reduce the damage from that attack by **2**, to a minimum of 0.

The attack still counts as having targeted you, even if all of its damage is prevented.

---

## Flashpowder

* **Type:** Tactical Reserve — Combat Tool
* **Use:** Action
* **Range:** Ranged
* **Target:** One enemy
* **Effect:** The target gains **Weakened**.

Draw or place the normal shared Weakened status card in the target's status row.

---

## Throwing Knife

* **Type:** Tactical Reserve — Thrown Weapon
* **Use:** Action
* **Range:** Ranged
* **Target:** One enemy
* **Effect:** Deal **1 physical damage** to the target.

If the target has one or more negative status cards, deal **+1 additional physical damage**.

This damage is not a weapon attack and does not benefit from weapon-attack modifiers.

---

# Dungeon Tools

## Skeleton Key

* **Type:** Tactical Reserve — Tool
* **Use:** Prepared Dungeon Effect
* **Effect:** Place this card in your status row.

When a **Locked Door** is revealed from the Dungeon Deck, you may bypass it.

Do not resolve the Locked Door. Place it in the Dungeon Discard Pile, then move Skeleton Key to the Tactical Reserve Discard Pile.

Skeleton Key cannot bypass a Boss card or a door that specifically says it cannot be opened by a key.

---

## Trapbreaker Kit

* **Type:** Tactical Reserve — Tool
* **Use:** Prepared Dungeon Effect
* **Effect:** Place this card in your status row.

When a **Trap** card is revealed from the Dungeon Deck, you may cancel that Trap.

Do not resolve the Trap. Place it in the Dungeon Discard Pile, then move Trapbreaker Kit to the Tactical Reserve Discard Pile.

---

## Surveyor's Scroll

* **Type:** Tactical Reserve — Scroll
* **Use:** Action
* **Effect:** Look at the top **two cards** of the Dungeon Deck.

Return those cards to the top of the Dungeon Deck in either order.

---

## Rope and Grapnel

* **Type:** Tactical Reserve — Tool
* **Use:** Prepared Dungeon Effect
* **Effect:** Place this card in your status row.

When an **Obstacle** or **Hazard** is revealed from the Dungeon Deck, you may bypass it.

Do not resolve that card. Place it in the Dungeon Discard Pile, then move Rope and Grapnel to the Tactical Reserve Discard Pile.

Rope and Grapnel cannot bypass a Boss card.

---

* **Type:** Tactical Reserve — Scroll
* **Use:** Action
* **Range:** Ranged
* **Target:** One enemy
* **Effect:** Deal **1 ice damage** to the target.

Then move that target **back 1 row**, if able.

---

## Scroll of Purification

* **Type:** Tactical Reserve — Scroll
* **Use:** Action
* **Range:** Ranged
* **Target:** Yourself or one ally
* **Effect:** Remove one negative status card from the target.

The removed status may be:

* Bleeding
* Burning
* One Poison status card
* Weakened
* Stunned

---

## Scroll of Warding

* **Type:** Tactical Reserve — Scroll
* **Use:** Reaction
* **Trigger:** You or one ally is about to take Fire, Ice, Lightning, Earth, Arcane, Necrotic, or Shadow damage.
* **Range:** Ranged
* **Target:** The Friendly Entity taking damage
* **Effect:** Prevent **2 damage** of that damage instance.

---

## Scroll of Relocation

* **Type:** Tactical Reserve — Scroll
* **Use:** Action
* **Range:** Ranged
* **Target:** Yourself or one ally
* **Effect:** Move the target **1 row** forward or back.

The target cannot move beyond the front or back row.

# Initial Balance Notes

* **Healing Potion** restores more HP than Heal Minor Wounds because it is single-use, must be carried into the dungeon, and normally requires same-row access.
* **Scroll of Mending** matches Healing Potion's healing but works at range. Its balancing cost is that it is a consumable magical resource rather than a physical bottle.
* **Focus Draught** is intentionally a Reaction because a Stunned character cannot use an Action card to cure their own Stunned status.
* **Smoke Bomb** is defensive but does not change Heat or prevent an enemy from selecting its target. It only reduces one incoming damage instance.
* **Trapbreaker Kit**, **Skeleton Key**, and **Rope and Grapnel** intentionally resemble weaker single-use versions of specialized Thief and Necromancy dungeon tools. Those classes remain better at repeated dungeon manipulation.
* **Acid Flask** has a one-round Defense penalty rather than creating a permanent Corroded status. This avoids adding a new shared negative-status card before that status is formally designed.
