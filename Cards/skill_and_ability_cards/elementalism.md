# Elementalism Skill and Spell Cards

## Elementalism Card Rules

### Casting Elementalism Spells

An Elementalism spell uses the caster's normal **shared activation**.

After resolving a spell, return it to its equipped spell slot beneath the appropriate Elementalism Skill Card unless the card instructs you to place it in a Friendly Entity's status row.

### Elemental Damage Types

Elementalism spells can deal the following damage types:

* Fire
* Ice
* Lightning
* Earth
* Arcane

**Arcane damage** is magical but is not an elemental damage type. It is intended to be useful against enemies that resist, ignore, or react strongly to Fire, Ice, Lightning, or Earth damage.

### Active Elemental Buffs

Some Elementalism spells create a positive status effect.

* Place the spell card in the target's status row.
* The effect lasts until its printed expiration condition.
* When the effect ends, return the spell card to its equipped spell slot.
* A Friendly Entity cannot have more than one active copy of the same named Elementalism buff.

### Moving Between Rows

When an Elementalism spell moves an entity:

* Move it one row forward or back, as specified by the spell.
* An entity cannot be moved beyond the front or back row.
* If an effect cannot move an entity because there is no legal row in that direction, resolve all other parts of the spell normally.

# Elementalism Skill Cards

## Novice Elementalism

* **Type:** Skill Card
* **Rank:** Novice
* **Equip Spell Slots:** 1

### Passive: Attuned Casting

Whenever one of your Fire, Ice, Lightning, or Earth spells deals damage to a target, the first **1 damage** dealt to that target by that spell ignores Defense.

---

## Apprentice Elementalism

* **Type:** Skill Card
* **Rank:** Apprentice
* **Equip Spell Slots:** 2

### Passive: Elemental Conversion

When you cast an Elementalism spell that would deal Fire, Ice, Lightning, or Earth damage, you may change all damage dealt by that spell to one other damage type from that list.

You cannot use this passive to change damage into Arcane damage.

---

## Adept Elementalism

* **Type:** Skill Card
* **Rank:** Adept
* **Equip Spell Slots:** 3

### Passive: Protective Resonance

A Friendly Entity with one of your active Elementalism buff cards in its status row takes **1 less elemental damage**, to a minimum of 0.

---

## Master Elementalism

* **Type:** Skill Card
* **Rank:** Master
* **Equip Spell Slots:** 3

### Passive: Cascading Force

Whenever one of your Elementalism spells deals damage to two or more enemies, each enemy damaged by that spell loses **1 Defense** until the end of the current round.

---

## Grandmaster Elementalism

* **Type:** Skill Card
* **Rank:** Grandmaster
* **Equip Spell Slots:** 4

### Passive: Perfect Attunement

Whenever one of your Elementalism spells deals **3 or more damage** to a single target, deal **+1 damage** to that target.

Apply this bonus before damage reduction.

# Elementalism Design Summary

* **Novice:** Reliable low-damage ranged elemental attacks and a short-lived defensive barrier.
* **Apprentice:** Stronger single-target damage, two-target lightning, movement control, and durable defense.
* **Adept:** Row attacks, hard-hitting targeted spells, conditional Earth damage, and elemental protection.
* **Master:** More powerful multi-target attacks, flexible damage selection, and substantial armor magic.
* **Grandmaster:** High-impact battlefield attacks, strong multi-target control, major defensive wards, and mixed-element damage.

# Initial Balance Notes

* **Fireball** is intentionally efficient against grouped enemies. Its main counterweight is that it requires the enemy row to contain multiple meaningful targets.
* **Ice Lance** becomes especially strong with Perfect Attunement, reaching 4 damage before mitigation at Grandmaster Elementalism.
* **Meteor** also benefits from Perfect Attunement on its primary target, making it 5 fire damage before mitigation. That is appropriate for a Grandmaster spell, but it should be watched during boss testing.
* **Elemental Conversion** gives Elementalism meaningful tactical flexibility without adding a separate spell for every possible resistance matchup.
