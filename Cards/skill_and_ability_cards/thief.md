I treated **Thief** as the Rogue’s precision-and-utility branch: dagger and light ranged attacks, Heat control, enemy debuffs, bounty marks, and prepared answers to traps and locked doors. Every delayed effect uses a card in a status row, so there is no sideways-card or memory-tax bookkeeping.

# Thief Card Rules

## Casting Thief Abilities

A Thief ability uses the Rogue's normal **shared activation** unless it is specifically identified as a Reaction.

When an ability resolves, return it to its equipped skill-card slot unless its text instructs you to place it in a Friendly Entity's or enemy's status row.

## Thief Weapon Requirements

When an ability instructs you to make a weapon attack, use the normal core weapon-attack rules.

* A **melee dagger weapon attack** requires an equipped weapon with both the **Melee** and **Dagger** tags.
* A **ranged Thief weapon attack** requires an equipped weapon with the **Ranged** tag and either the **Shortbow** or **Crossbow** subtype tag.
* A Thief ability that says “make a weapon attack” does not create a separate damage system. It uses the equipped weapon's printed damage and naturally generates Heat under the core combat rules.

## Active Thief Techniques

Some Thief abilities become ongoing cards.

* Place the card in the listed entity's status row.
* The card remains there until its printed expiration condition is met.
* When the card ends, return it to its equipped slot.
* An enemy can have more than one different Thief technique in its status row, but cannot have more than one active copy of the same named technique.

## Prepared Dungeon Techniques

Some Thief abilities are cast during combat but wait to resolve when the Dungeon Deck reveals a matching card.

* Cast the ability using the Rogue's shared activation.
* Place the ability card in the Rogue's status row.
* When its listed Dungeon Deck trigger occurs, the Rogue may resolve the effect immediately.
* After resolving, return the card to its equipped slot.
* If the Rogue does not use the effect when its matching trigger occurs, the card remains active for a later matching trigger.
* A Rogue cannot have more than one active copy of the same Prepared Dungeon Technique.

## Defeated Enemies

An enemy is defeated only when it is removed from the battlefield and placed in the **Dungeon Discard Pile**.

An enemy that flees, is removed from combat by another effect, or otherwise leaves the battlefield without entering the Dungeon Discard Pile does not count as defeated.

# Thief Design Summary

* **Cutpurse:** Basic dagger exploitation, currency from kills, Heat reduction, and locked-door preparation.
* **Burglar:** Ranged Thief combat, Weakened infliction, trap avoidance, and temporary target-selection control.
* **Infiltrator:** High-value dagger attacks, armor-breaking, bounty targets, and advanced trap handling.
* **Shadow:** Strong status-exploitation attacks, major stealth movement, broad dungeon-card bypassing, and loot preparation.
* **Master Thief:** Execution attacks, elite ranged damage, near-total temporary disappearance from enemy targeting, and high-value loot or bounty manipulation.

# Initial Balance Watchpoints

* **Backstab** can become very strong when Cutpurse Training, Infiltrator Training, and Master Thief Training are all equipped. Against a negative-status enemy at half HP, it can receive several stacked bonuses. That is appropriate for specialization, but it should be tested against elite enemies and bosses.
* **Master Lockpick** and **Perfect Heist** are intentionally powerful because they consume the Rogue's shared activation in an earlier combat round and may not trigger immediately.
* **Grand Larceny** should remain unable to target Boss enemies unless testing shows boss bounty rewards are safe for the economy.
* **Ghostwalk** is stronger than Smoke Screen because it blocks default enemy targeting instead of merely lowering effective Heat. It should remain limited to the current round and should not stop enemy abilities that explicitly choose the Rogue as their target.
