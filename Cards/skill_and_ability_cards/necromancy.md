I made the lower-rank death-trigger spells into **one-round gambits**, while their stronger versions become **persistent enemy-bound Omen cards**. The other dungeon-manipulation spells are now **prepared during combat** and wait in the caster’s status row for the relevant dungeon card to appear.

# Necromancy Skill and Spell Cards

## Core Necromancy Rules

### Necromancy Spell Timing

There is no separate exploration-action phase.

Unless a card specifically says it is a **Reaction**, a Necromancy spell must be cast using the caster's normal **shared activation** during combat. If the player character casts a spell, that character has used its shared activation for the turn and cannot also activate a Controlled Ally.

### Defeated Enemy

For these cards, an enemy is **defeated** only when it is removed from the battlefield and placed into the **Dungeon Discard Pile**.

An enemy that leaves the battlefield for another reason, such as fleeing, being removed by a special effect, being returned to the Dungeon Deck, or otherwise leaving without being defeated, does **not** count as defeated.

### One-Round Death Trigger

Some Necromancy spells are cast during combat and wait for a death that round.

* Cast the spell using the caster's shared activation.
* Place the spell card face-up beside the caster.
* The spell triggers when the **next enemy is defeated after the spell is cast**, before the current round ends.
* If no enemy is defeated before the round ends, return the spell card to its owner with no effect.

### Omen Cards

Some Necromancy spells are persistent curses or omens.

* When cast, place the spell card in the chosen enemy's status row.
* The card remains there until that enemy is defeated or leaves the battlefield.
* If the enemy is defeated, resolve the card's effect immediately.
* If the enemy leaves the battlefield for any reason other than being defeated, return the spell card to its owner without resolving its death effect.
* Once placed, an Omen card resolves normally even if its caster later becomes Stunned.

### Prepared Dungeon Effects

Some spells create a prepared effect rather than resolving immediately.

* Cast the spell during combat using the caster's shared activation.
* Place the spell card in the caster's status row.
* The card remains there until its listed dungeon trigger occurs.
* When that trigger occurs, resolve the effect and return the spell card to its owner.
* A caster may have only **one copy of each named Prepared Dungeon Effect** active at a time.

### Expend a Defeated Enemy

When a card tells you to **expend a defeated enemy**, choose one enemy card in the Dungeon Discard Pile and set it aside outside the current dungeon.

That enemy is no longer in the Dungeon Discard Pile, cannot return to the Dungeon Deck, and cannot be used as the cost of another effect during this dungeon.

### Summon Cards

A summon card is both the spell and the summoned Friendly Entity.

* When cast, place the card in its listed battlefield row.
* The summon is a **Controlled Ally** owned by the caster.
* It uses the normal shared activation rules.
* When the summon leaves the battlefield, return the card to its owner unless another effect says otherwise.

## Tomb Pact

* **Rank:** Grandmaster
* **Type:** Spell — Prepared Dungeon Effect
* **Effect:** Place this card in the caster's status row.

When an Obstacle, Trap, Locked Door, or Hazard is revealed from the Dungeon Deck, you may return up to **two defeated enemies** from the Dungeon Discard Pile to the Dungeon Deck and shuffle that deck.

If you return:

* **One enemy:** Bypass the revealed card.
* **Two enemies:** Bypass the revealed card, then look at the top two cards of the Dungeon Deck and place them back in either order.

Do not resolve the bypassed card. Place it in the Dungeon Discard Pile.

Return **Tomb Pact** to its owner after it resolves.

Tomb Pact cannot bypass a Boss card.

## King's Ransom

* **Rank:** Grandmaster
* **Type:** Spell — Omen
* **Range:** Ranged
* **Target:** One enemy
* **Effect:** Place this card in the target's status row.

When that enemy is defeated:

1. Gain **5 currency**.
2. Draw or claim the next Loot card revealed from the Dungeon Deck into the Encounter Stash.
3. Return King's Ransom to its owner.

If the target leaves the battlefield without being defeated, return this card to its owner with no effect.

> **Optional Restriction:** If this proves too profitable against bosses or elite enemies, add: “King's Ransom cannot target a Boss.”

The main balance pressure point is **Skeletal Pyromancer**. Three ranged fire damage every time you choose its shared activation could outpace most Apprentice spells, even with the cost of summoning and protecting it.
